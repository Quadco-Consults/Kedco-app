COMPLIANCE WITH REGULATORY DIRECTIVES ON SERVICE DELIVERY STANDARDS

We acknowledge receipt of your letter referenced NERC/ABJ/2025/QRY/089 dated October 15, 2025, regarding concerns raised about service delivery standards and customer complaint resolution in KEDCO's franchise area. We appreciate the Commission's commitment to ensuring quality service delivery to electricity consumers.

We wish to provide the following comprehensive response to the queries raised in your letter:

1. Customer Complaint Resolution Timeline

KEDCO acknowledges that there have been delays in resolving certain customer complaints within the stipulated 48-hour timeframe as required by the Customer Service Standards. We have conducted an internal review and identified the following challenges:

  • Inadequate manpower in certain service centers during peak periods
  • Technical limitations in our customer complaint management system
  • Complex technical faults requiring extended resolution times

Remedial Actions Taken:

  • Recruitment of 45 additional customer service personnel (completed September 2025)
  • Upgrade of our complaint management software with automated tracking (ongoing - 80% complete)
  • Establishment of specialized technical response teams in 6 operational zones
  • Implementation of escalation matrix for complaints exceeding 24 hours

2. Metering Gap and Estimated Billing

We acknowledge the Commission's concern regarding the metering gap in our franchise area. As of September 30, 2025:

  • Total registered customers: 847,523
  • Metered customers: 521,089 (61.5%)
  • Unmetered customers: 326,434 (38.5%)

Metering Acceleration Plan:

  • Target deployment of 150,000 meters in Q4 2025 and Q1 2026
  • Partnership with three meter vendors under the Meter Asset Provider (MAP) scheme
  • Procurement of 50,000 meters through CAPEX allocation
  • Expected reduction of metering gap to 18% by June 2026

3. Network Performance and Service Reliability

KEDCO reports the following network performance metrics for Q3 2025:

  • Average supply hours per day: 16.8 hours (target: 18 hours)
  • System Availability: 82.4% (target: 85%)
  • Total Technical and Commercial Losses: 23.7% (target: 22%)
  • Collection Efficiency: 78.3% (target: 80%)

We are implementing targeted interventions to improve these metrics, including network reinforcement projects in 12 high-loss areas and deployment of prepaid meters to improve collection efficiency.

4. Commitment to Regulatory Compliance

KEDCO reaffirms its commitment to full compliance with all NERC regulations and service standards. We have instituted a Regulatory Compliance Unit directly under the Managing Director's office to ensure continuous monitoring and reporting of all regulatory obligations.

We respectfully request the Commission to:

  • Accept our proposed improvement timelines as outlined above
  • Schedule a joint technical review meeting to discuss infrastructure investment plans
  • Consider our challenges in implementing certain standards due to funding constraints

5. Conclusion

KEDCO appreciates the Commission's regulatory oversight and is committed to improving service delivery to all customers within our franchise area. We will submit monthly progress reports on the implementation of the remedial actions outlined above, commencing November 2025.

We remain available for any clarifications or additional information the Commission may require.
